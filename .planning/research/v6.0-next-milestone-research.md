# What to Build Next — v6.0 Milestone Research

**Date:** 2026-06-05
**Question:** Given where the product is (v1.0–v5.0 shipped), what's the highest-value next milestone?
**Method:** 5 parallel research agents (AI-video/previz, competitor gaps, export/collab demand, API-vs-app strategy, indie pain points). Sources cited inline.

---

## Headline

Two findings dominate and they point in different directions:

1. **Export (.fdx + standard-format PDF + breakdown sheets) is a hard adoption blocker.** Every serious competitor reads/writes `.fdx`; contests/agents/actors only accept standard-format PDF; ADs need PDF breakdown sheets. The format is open and cheap to implement. The app is currently an "island."
2. **AI previz (storyboard frame → animated shot) is the strategically differentiating move** — it's the one thing this app is uniquely positioned to own (it already has storyboard frames + Imagen), it's the workflow indies most welcome AI for ("do my dishes" zone), and no incumbent owns the full write→breakdown→previz chain.

These aren't either/or — they're a sequence. Export is the *floor* (removes a blocker); previz is the *ceiling* (builds the moat).

---

## The strategic fork: API-platform vs. end-user app → **stay an app** (high confidence ~80%)

- The AI-screenwriting market is small (~$220M 2025) and **app-shaped** — no one wins as a third-party "breakdown API." No visible developer demand pulling for it. [Studiovity](https://blog.studiovity.com/top-ai-scriptwriting-tools-for-filmmakers-in-2026/)
- v5.0's API keys are best read as a **BYOK/power-user feature**, not a pivot — same kit JetBrains/Vercel/OpenRouter shipped in 2025. [JetBrains BYOK](https://blog.jetbrains.com/ai/2025/12/bring-your-own-key-byok-is-now-live-in-jetbrains-ides/)
- "Platform before product" is a documented antipattern; an API would *commoditize* the breakdown — the most valuable middle of the pipeline. [itamarnovick](https://www.itamarnovick.com/startup-anti-pattern-3-platform-risk/)
- **The integrated write→breakdown→shotlist→storyboard→previz pipeline IS the moat.** Marginal value is far higher deepening it than building developer infra.

→ Do NOT pivot to a platform. Keep clean API boundaries as a cheap option, productize BYOK for existing users, but invest cycles in pipeline depth.

## Candidate v6.0 milestones (prioritized)

### Candidate A — Industry-Format Export & Interoperability `[FLOOR / table-stakes]`
**User value:** The app stops being an island. Scripts leave as `.fdx` (round-trips to Final Draft/StudioBinder/Yamdu), as standard-format PDF (contests, agents, actors, sides), and breakdown data leaves as PDF breakdown sheets the 1st AD carries on set.
**Why now:** `.fdx` is the lingua franca every competitor supports ([Filmustage .fdx](https://help.filmustage.com/en/articles/6523169-export-script-to-final-draft-fdx), [StudioBinder import](https://support.studiobinder.com/en/articles/10952562-how-to-import-your-screenplay)). Contests universally require standard-format PDF ([Final Draft Big Break](https://www.finaldraft.com/big-break-screenwriting-contest/), [Raindance](https://raindance.org/festival/submit/scripts/)). Without these the app **cannot reach contests, agents, or real productions** — verified as a hard blocker.
**Leverage vs net-new:** High leverage. `.fdx` is plain documented XML the app can emit directly ([fileformats](http://fileformats.archiveteam.org/wiki/Final_Draft)); breakdown data already exists in the DB — this is serialization + a PDF formatter, not new product surface.
**Complexity:** Low–Medium. Main work: correct screenplay-PDF formatting (12pt Courier, industry margins) and faithful `.fdx` emission.
**Risk:** Low. Mostly "boring but essential." Edge cases in PDF pagination/formatting fidelity.

### Candidate B — AI Previz: Storyboard → Animated Shot `[CEILING / differentiator]`
**User value:** Turn existing storyboard frames into short animated previz clips per shot — disposable, controllable planning scaffolding (not final art). Compresses a previously cost-prohibitive line item to hours.
**Why now:** Image-to-video (start-frame) is first-class across every major model in 2025–26 — exactly matching the app's storyboard frames as conditioning input ([Veo/Kling/Runway pricing](https://modelslab.com/blog/api/veo-3-1-vs-kling-3-sora-2-ai-video-api-cost-2026)). Katalist/LTX prove "frame → animated shot" as a shippable pattern, not research ([Katalist](https://www.katalist.ai/)). This is the **#1 AI use indies actively welcome** — pre-production prep is the "I want AI to do my dishes" zone ([No Film School](https://nofilmschool.com/how-indie-filmmakers-can-survive-ai-9-insights-from-pros), [Hollywood Reporter](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/business/business-news/inside-indie-films-ai-reckoning-1236424907/)).
**Leverage vs net-new:** Medium-high. Storyboard data model + Imagen frames + media pipeline already exist. Net-new: video-provider integration (best via **model-agnostic aggregator like fal.ai/Replicate** — hedges Sora's Sep-2026 API sunset), async job UX, credit metering.
**Complexity:** Medium. Async generate-and-wait jobs, credit/billing metering, model selection. ~$10–11 per 30-shot previz pass on Kling — meter it, don't make it free.
**Risk:** Medium. Cost control, consistency drift across shots (mitigated by reference-locking), and **AI-backlash positioning** — must frame as controllable disposable previz the filmmaker steers, never as final imagery ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/20/ais-promise-to-indie-filmmakers-faster-cheaper-lonelier/)).

### Candidate C — Breakdown → Schedule Bridge (Stripboard + DOOD + Call Sheets) `[depth, pro-adoption]`
**User value:** Turn the breakdown into a drag-and-drop stripboard, auto-generate Day-Out-of-Days, and build call sheets — the operational bridge from "what's in the script" to "what we shoot Tuesday."
**Why now:** Every production competitor ships this (Filmustage, StudioBinder, Yamdu, Celtx); it's the critical missing bridge between the app's breakdown and actual production. ([StudioBinder scheduling](https://www.studiobinder.com/film-scheduling-software/))
**Leverage vs net-new:** Lower leverage — largely net-new product surface (scheduling engine, stripboard UI, call-sheet generation/delivery).
**Complexity:** High. Biggest build of the four.
**Risk:** Medium-high. Historically marked out-of-scope ("different product domain"); pulls toward StudioBinder/Filmustage territory where they're entrenched. Story-order↔setup-order sorting is the genuinely valuable nugget here.

### Candidate D — Real-Time Collaboration (shared projects + comments + roles) `[important, sequence after export]`
**User value:** Director/DP/AD co-edit the shotlist/storyboard live; comments instead of email chains; shareable read-only links.
**Why now:** Real-time collab is now baseline for WriterDuet/Arc Studio/Celtx ([Arc Studio](https://www.arcstudiopro.com/)). The breakdown+previz workflow is *inherently* multi-role (shotlist "has always been a collaboration between director, cinematographer, and 1st AD" — [StudioBinder](https://www.studiobinder.com/blog/how-to-make-a-shot-list/)).
**Leverage vs net-new:** Medium. Auth/user model exist; net-new: sharing, membership, comments, presence (real-time sync is the hard part).
**Complexity:** Medium-High (real-time infra).
**Risk:** Medium. Important but not a day-one blocker — a solo writer survives single-user. MVP scope (shared links + comments + membership) before roles/revision history.

---

## Recommendation

**Ship Candidate A (Export) first, then Candidate B (AI Previz) — ideally bundle a lean A into the B milestone, or run A as a fast warm-up.**

Reasoning:
- **A is the floor you can't skip.** It's verified as a hard adoption blocker, it's cheap (open format, data already in DB), and it unblocks every real-world user (contests, agents, ADs, interop with the tools they already use). Shipping previz on top of an app nobody can get scripts *out* of is building the penthouse before the front door.
- **B is the ceiling that makes the product special.** It's the one move uniquely yours — you already own the hard input (storyboard frames), the model landscape just made it cheap and API-accessible, and it's the exact AI use indies *welcome* rather than resent. It extends the moat (the full write→breakdown→previz chain no incumbent owns) instead of chasing StudioBinder on its home turf (Candidate C).
- **Defer C** — highest effort, lowest leverage, historically out-of-scope, entrenched competition. Cherry-pick only the story↔setup-order shotlist toggle if anything.
- **Sequence D after** — important and inherently fits the workflow, but survivable to defer; do MVP scope when you get to it.

**One-line strategy:** *"Stop re-typing your script into five tools"* — own the single data spine from script → breakdown → shotlist → storyboard → animated previz, make it leave cleanly in industry formats, and frame AI as the dishwasher (disposable previz the filmmaker controls), never the auteur.
